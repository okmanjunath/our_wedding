How Do I Get There?

The nearest airports to the wedding venue are:

  • ✈️ Cochin International Airport (COK) – Approximately 85 km away,
    (probably the best option: cheaper flights, good connections to Palakkad)
  • ✈️ Calicut International Airport (CCJ) – Approximately 70 km away
  • ✈️ Coimbatore International Airport (CJB) – Approximately 110 km away

Travel Tips

  • Airport Taxi: If you are heading directly from the airport to the hotel near the wedding venue, we recommend taking an airport taxi. The cost is approximately €30-40. You can easily book a prepaid taxi at the airport – there is usually a prepaid taxi counter near the exit. This is the most convenient and hassle-free option.

  • Transportation to the Wedding Venue: For guests staying in the accommodation we have arranged, transportation to and from the wedding/ reception venue will be provided.

  • Local Travel: For travel within major cities like Kochi, Calicut, or Trivandrum, you can use local taxis, Uber, or Ola, which are readily available. Auto-rickshaws (tuk-tuks) are also a great option for short distances. Be sure to agree on the rate before you start your journey to avoid any misunderstandings.

  • If you plan on visiting multiple places in Kerala for at least a couple of days, you can hire a single taxi for your entire trip to ensure a smooth and hassle-free experience. We especially recommend this if you’re planning a trip to hill stations like Munnar or Ooty, as having a dedicated taxi makes travel more convenient and comfortable.

  • If you prefer traveling by train, we suggest looking for Vande Bharat Express, a semi-high-speed train that connects major cities with modern facilities. However, tickets need to be booked in advance through the IRCTC website.

  • If you're traveling as a group and need assistance booking a reliable taxi, let us know – we’d be happy to arrange it for you!

What Can I Wear?

There is no strict dress code. Feel free to wear a nice shirt and trousers, a skirt with a stylish top, or something similar you're comfortable in. Just a heads-up—suits and blazers aren't really common at Indian weddings, as you might have guessed! If you're excited to try something different and want to blend in with the crowd, here are some options below. You will notice that certain outfits appear in both the wedding and reception sections of the reference images, as they are flexible enough to be worn for either event.

Wedding


For Men:
  • Traditional Option:Mundu (white/off-white dhoti with a gold/colored border) paired with a Kurta or shirt matching the mundu border.
    (Avoid golden/white/off-white kurtas, as they are for the groom.)

  • Alternative Option: Formal/ semi-casual shirt paired with trousers (pants).
For Women:
  • Traditional Option 1: A Kasavu Saree or a traditional silk saree.

  • Traditional Option 2: An Anarkali churidhaar or a salwar kameez.

  • Jewelry & Accessories: Gold imitation jewelry (usually the bride wears gold), jasmine flowers in the hair, and bangles to complete the look.

Get Ready to Experience the Magic of Kerala’s Monsoon! ☔🌿

You’ll be visiting us during Kerala’s enchanting monsoon season—a time when the land transforms into a lush paradise, with mist-covered hills, flowing waterfalls, and the rhythmic sound of raindrops adding to its charm. The rains breathe new life into the landscapes, making it the perfect season to experience Kerala’s natural beauty, vibrant culture, and rich traditions.

Explore the Best of Kerala

While the beaches may not be at their best, there’s so much more to explore! From serene backwaters and breathtaking hill stations to ancient forts, dense rainforests, and stunning waterfalls , Kerala offers a truly unforgettable experience. You can also immerse yourself in the region’s arts and culture, witnessing classical dance performances like Kathakali, mesmerizing percussion ensembles, and grand temple festivals.

Relax & Rejuvenate with Ayurveda

Monsoon is also considered the best time for Ayurvedic rejuvenation, making it an ideal season for wellness retreats, and relaxing spa experiences. Kerala is home to world-renowned Ayurveda resorts and luxury spas that offer treatments designed to refresh both body and mind. Whether it's a soothing herbal oil massage or a calming spa session, this is the perfect opportunity to unwind and revitalize.

Must-Visit Nearby Attractions

These are just some of the incredible places you can explore, but there are many more hidden gems waiting to be discovered. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, Kerala has it all!

🌾 Kollengode

Considered the most beautiful village in India, known for its serene landscapes, Kollengode Palace, and lush greenery.

🏰 Varikkasseri Mana

A historic Kerala mansion that showcases traditional architecture and cultural heritage.

🌊 Silent Valley National Park

A UNESCO-listed rainforest teeming with rare wildlife.

🐅 Parambikulam Tiger Reserve

A lush wildlife sanctuary home to tigers, elephants, and exotic bird species.

💦 Dhoni Waterfalls

A hidden gem where a short trek leads to a stunning monsoon-fed waterfall.

🎭 Kerala Kalamandalam

The heart of Kerala’s classical performing arts, including Kathakali and Mohiniyattam.

🎶 Malampuzha Dam & The Yakshi

A scenic getaway featuring the majestic Malampuzha Dam and the iconic Yakshi sculpture, set amidst beautifully landscaped gardens.

🏞️ Nelliyampathy Hills

A hill station with misty landscapes, tea plantations, and breathtaking viewpoints.

🌊 Athirapilly Waterfalls

Kerala’s largest and most spectacular waterfall, especially mesmerizing in the monsoon.

Iconic Destinations in & Around Kerala

Kerala is home to a fascinating mix of modern cities, serene backwaters, and breathtaking hill stations. Whether you're exploring vibrant urban life or seeking a peaceful retreat, here are some incredible destinations to consider:

🏙️ Kochi

A lively port city blending colonial heritage, bustling markets, and modern cafes.

🏛️ Thiruvananthapuram

Kerala’s capital, home to historic temples, museums, and stunning beaches.

🌊 Kovalam

A world-famous beach destination known for golden sands and Ayurvedic retreats.

⛰️ Munnar

A picturesque hill station with sprawling tea plantations and misty mountains.

🐘 Thekkady

Famous for Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, home to elephants and boat safaris.

⛵ Kumarakom

A peaceful village on Vembanad Lake, offering houseboat cruises and birdwatching.

🛶 Alleppey Houseboats

Glide through the scenic Kerala backwaters on a traditional houseboat.

🌄 Ooty

A charming hill station in Tamil Nadu, perfect for cool weather and nature walks.

🌿 Coorg

Known as the ‘Scotland of India,’ Coorg boasts coffee plantations and waterfalls.


Whether you're here just for the wedding or planning a longer getaway, Kerala’s monsoon magic, cultural vibrance, and rejuvenating experiences will make your trip truly special!
